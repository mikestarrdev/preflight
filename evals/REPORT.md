### Reading the recall number

Recall looks catastrophic but the failure is narrow. Every one of the 19
misses *did* flag the ad as a violation — the adjudicator cited the
`example_violating` chunk (the ❌ example whose text matches the ad) instead
of the governing rule chunk the dataset expects. Measured at "did the run
flag the case at all", detection is 22/22.

So the baseline problem is citation targeting, not detection: retrieval
surfaces the ❌ example as the top hit because it is nearly identical to the
input, and the adjudicator quotes it. Candidate fixes for Phase 4 (not
applied here, so the before/after is real): have the adjudicator prefer
`rule` chunks when an example and its rule are both retrieved, attach
example chunks to their governing rule at retrieval time, or score a
citation of a section's example as a hit for its rule.

The one citation-accuracy drop was a quote stitched from a different chunk
than the one cited (`meta:deceptive-practices:2.1`), caught by verification
and dropped, as designed.
